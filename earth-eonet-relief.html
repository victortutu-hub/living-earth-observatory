Data sources
  • NASA EONET: near real-time natural event metadata.
  • GDACS: EU JRC / UN OCHA global disaster alerts. In the public demo it is explicit proxy-only fallback; locally it can be used as smart fallback or GDACS-only source.
  • NASA FIRMS: MODIS/VIIRS wildfire hotspots, optional supplemental fire layer requiring the local proxy and FIRMS_MAP_KEY.
  • NASA GIBS: daily cloud snapshot layer.
  • NOAA SWPC OVATION: aurora oval forecast intensity.
  • USGS: earthquake feed integrated into Earthquakes.
  • NOAA ETOPO1-derived relief: terrain and bathymetry reference.
  • Astronomy Engine: apparent Sun position and Moon position/phase geometry.
  • Astronomy Engine: real lunar libration (Astronomy.Libration) and real north-pole axis (Astronomy.RotationAxis) drive the Moon's actual visible-hemisphere orientation and disc roll.
  • Moon color/normal maps: Clementine/LRO satellite mosaic (default, CoryG89) or NASA LROC WAC (public domain, toggle available).
  • Moon relief: NASA SVS CGI Moon Kit — LOLA laser altimeter elevation data (local asset, NASA's CDN blocks direct CORS fetch).
  • CelesTrak + satellite.js (SGP4): ISS orbital position, altitude and velocity.
  • Airglow: real upper-atmosphere emission represented procedurally, OI green-dominant, not a live intensity snapshot.
  • Zodiacal light: sunlight scattered by interplanetary dust, approximated along the real Sun direction and ecliptic plane.
  • Noctilucent clouds: physically constrained procedural mesospheric layer, limited by latitude, season and twilight geometry; not a live morphology snapshot.
